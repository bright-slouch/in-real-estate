## Full Needs Discovery Questionnaire

### Section A: Buyer Background

1. What's bringing you to the market right now?
2. Have you bought a home before? If yes: when, where, and what did you learn from that experience?
3. Are you currently renting? Owning? When does that situation change or expire?
4. Who else is involved in this decision? (spouse, partner, family member, advisor)
5. Have you worked with a buyer's agent before? What was that experience like?

### Section B: Property Requirements

**Type and Size**
6. What type of property are you looking for? (single-family, townhome, condo, multi-family, other)
7. Minimum bedrooms you'd accept? Your ideal number?
8. Minimum bathrooms? Full baths vs half baths?
9. Do you need a garage? How many stalls? Attached or detached OK?
10. Square footage: is there a minimum that would feel too small? A range you'd prefer?
11. Basement: required, nice-to-have, or no preference?
12. One-story or two-story preference? Any accessibility needs?

**Condition and Age**
13. New construction, resale, or either?
14. Are you open to a fixer-upper if the price reflects it, or do you need move-in ready?
15. Biggest condition concerns: roof, HVAC, foundation, other?

**Features**
16. List up to 5 must-have features (items that would make you not write an offer).
17. List up to 5 nice-to-have features (important but not deal-breakers).
18. List up to 3 deal-breakers (features that would end your interest immediately).

Common must-haves to prompt on if buyer is vague:
- Main floor laundry
- Primary bedroom on main floor
- Fenced yard (pets/children)
- Home office or dedicated flex space
- Large kitchen / open floor plan
- Specific garage size
- Pool or no pool (HOA communities)
- Acreage / rural character

### Section C: Location

19. What counties or cities are you targeting?
20. School district: is this a requirement? Which districts are acceptable?
    - *Agent note: In Hamilton County especially, school district boundaries are value boundaries. Carmel Clay, Hamilton Southeastern, Westfield, Noblesville, and Zionsville all carry distinct premiums. Confirm this is on the buyer's radar.*
21. Where do you or your household commute to?
    - *Tip: Identify major employers (Salesforce/downtown Indy, Lilly on the south side, Cummins in Columbus, IU Health campuses, Elanco in Greenfield) and run commute time from target neighborhoods.*
22. Maximum commute time you're comfortable with?
23. Are there specific neighborhoods or subdivisions you've driven through and liked?
24. Are there areas you'd prefer to avoid?
25. Proximity needs beyond work: family, church, specific grocery stores, schools for existing children?
26. If your ideal area is over budget, would you consider adjacent neighborhoods?

### Section D: Lifestyle Factors

27. Do you have pets? What kind? (Relevant for HOA rules, yard needs, pet restrictions in condos)
28. Children currently or planned? (School quality, yard, room count, neighborhood character)
29. Work from home regularly? Need a dedicated office?
30. Do you entertain often? (Open floor plan, outdoor space, kitchen size)
31. Outdoor space: small patio fine, or need a meaningful yard?
32. How important is walkability vs driving everywhere?
33. Any mobility or accessibility requirements?

### Section E: Timeline and Motivation

34. When would you ideally like to be in a new home?
35. Is there a hard deadline? (Lease expiration, job start date, school enrollment, relocation package expiration)
36. How long have you been actively looking? Have you toured homes yet?
37. Have you made any offers that didn't work out? What happened?
38. On a scale of 1–10, how motivated are you to buy in the next 90 days?

### Section F: Financial Readiness

39. Have you been pre-approved for a mortgage? If yes: lender name, loan type, pre-approval amount?
40. If not pre-approved: have you reviewed your credit score recently?
41. Down payment: what percentage or dollar amount are you planning to put down?
42. Are you aware of down payment assistance programs available in Indiana?
43. What monthly payment range are you comfortable with?
44. Do you have additional funds for closing costs (~$3,000–$7,000 typical in Indiana)?
45. Are there any credit or income factors I should know about that might affect your financing?
46. If you own a home to sell: does that sale need to close before you can buy? Have you talked to your agent/lender about bridge loan options?

## Pre-Approval Checklist

Provide this to buyers who are not yet pre-approved:

**Documents Most Lenders Require:**
- [ ] W-2s for the last 2 years (all employers)
- [ ] Federal tax returns for the last 2 years (all pages, signed)
- [ ] Most recent 30 days of pay stubs
- [ ] Most recent 2 months of bank statements (all accounts, all pages — no blacked-out lines)
- [ ] Most recent retirement/investment account statements (if using for down payment)
- [ ] Government-issued photo ID
- [ ] Social security number (for credit pull authorization)
- [ ] Explanation letters for: any large deposits, gaps in employment, credit inquiries, derogatory credit items
- If self-employed: 2 years business tax returns + year-to-date P&L
- If military/VA: Certificate of Eligibility (COE), DD-214 if separated

**Timeline Expectation:**
- Basic pre-qualification: same day
- Full pre-approval with full document review: 3–7 business days
- VA COE if not already obtained: 1–3 business days (lender can pull via VA portal)

## Loan Type Quick Reference

### Conventional (Fannie Mae / Freddie Mac)
- Down payment: 3–20%
- PMI required if < 20% down; cancelable once equity reaches 20%
- Credit score: minimum 620; best rates at 740+
- DTI: typically 45% max; up to 50% with compensating factors
- Property condition: must meet basic habitability standards; no major deferred maintenance
- Best for: buyers with decent credit, stable income, and at least 3–5% down saved

### FHA (Federal Housing Administration)
- Down payment: 3.5% (with 580+ credit); 10% (with 500–579 credit)
- Upfront MIP: 1.75% of loan amount (can be financed)
- Annual MIP: 0.55–0.85% depending on term and LTV; NOT cancelable if < 10% down
- Credit score: 580+ for 3.5% down
- DTI: up to 57% in some cases with strong compensating factors
- Property condition: FHA appraisers flag health/safety issues; avoid "fixer" properties
- Best for: first-time buyers with limited down payment or moderate credit

### VA (Veterans Affairs)
- Down payment: 0% for most eligible buyers
- No PMI; VA funding fee instead (1.25–3.3% depending on usage; waived for disabled vets)
- Credit score: no VA minimum; most lenders require 620+
- DTI: guideline is 41%; lenders have discretion with residual income formula
- VA appraisal required: VA-assigned appraiser; VA MPRs (Minimum Property Requirements) must be met
- Eligible: veterans, active duty, National Guard (with 6 years qualifying service), certain surviving spouses
- Indiana note: VA loans work well here; no state-specific complications; confirm COE early
- Best for: any eligible veteran — almost always the best financial deal available

### USDA (Rural Development)
- Down payment: 0%
- Upfront guarantee fee: 1% of loan amount (financeable)
- Annual fee: 0.35% of outstanding balance
- Property must be in USDA-designated eligible rural area — check eligibility map: eligibility.sc.egov.usda.gov
- Income limits apply (moderate income limit, typically 115% of area median)
- Indiana USDA-eligible areas near Central Indiana: outer Boone County (west of Lebanon), outer Morgan County, outer Shelby County, eastern Hendricks County, outer Madison County
- Best for: buyers targeting rural/exurban areas who qualify income-wise; 0% down without VA eligibility

### IHCDA Programs (Indiana Housing and Community Development Authority)

**First Home / Next Home Down Payment Assistance:**
- Provides 3–3.5% down payment assistance as forgivable second mortgage (forgiven after 2 years if primary stays in place)
- Must use an IHCDA-participating lender (list maintained by IHCDA; check current list)
- Income limits: vary by county and household size; for Hamilton County typically $90K–$110K household income cap
- Purchase price limits: vary by county; Hamilton County cap approximately $375K–$450K (check current IHCDA limits — these update)
- First Home: buyer cannot have owned a home in the past 3 years (or must be buying in a targeted area)
- Next Home: available to move-up buyers without the 3-year ownership restriction
- Requires homebuyer education course (HUD-approved; available online, $30–$75)

**MCC (Mortgage Credit Certificate):**
- Federal tax credit of up to 25% of annual mortgage interest paid
- Reduces federal income tax liability dollar-for-dollar
- Example: on a $250K mortgage at 7%, annual interest ~$17,500; 25% MCC = $4,375 tax credit per year
- Stacks with IHCDA down payment assistance
- Available to qualifying first-time buyers through participating lenders

## Buyer Scenario Decision Trees

### First-Time Buyer with Limited Savings
1. Credit 580+, savings ≥ 3.5% + closing costs → FHA as starting point; run conventional comparison
2. Credit 620+, savings < 5% → Consider IHCDA DPA; stack with conventional 3%
3. Income within IHCDA limits → Always check IHCDA eligibility
4. Eligible for MCC → Layer on top of DPA for maximum tax benefit
5. No savings at all → VA if eligible; USDA if rural target; otherwise education needed before buying

### Move-Up Buyer with Home to Sell
1. Does sale need to close before purchase? → Home sale contingency in offer; discuss with lender about bridge options
2. Can buyer qualify without selling? → Avoid contingent offer — much stronger position
3. Timeline overlap (selling first) → Temporary housing plan? Consider rental gap
4. Timeline overlap (buying first) → Two mortgages briefly; lender must qualify buyer carrying both

### VA Buyer
1. COE obtained? → If not: direct to lender immediately — they pull via VA portal in 1 day
2. Disability rating? → Funding fee waiver if any service-connected disability rating
3. Prior VA loan paid off? → Full entitlement restored
4. Prior VA loan not paid off? → Remaining entitlement only; may need down payment above $144K entitlement limit
5. Property condition? → VA MPRs flag health/safety defects; avoid properties with significant deferred maintenance in MO 1–2 offers

### Relocation Buyer from Out of State
1. Not pre-approved locally → Indiana lender may have more competitive rates than out-of-state lender; offer comparison
2. Unfamiliar with Indiana geography → Run re-neighborhood-research for all target areas; include commute data
3. Can't tour in person → Virtual showing via ShowingTime; video walkthroughs; re-showing-coordinator for remote setup
4. Relocation package with specific lender → Agent cannot control; provide Indiana-specific guidance around their program

## Consultation Flow (60-Minute Appointment)

| Time Block | Topic |
|---|---|
| 0–5 min | Welcome, coffee/water, agenda overview |
| 5–20 min | Discovery questions (Section A–E above) |
| 20–30 min | Financial overview and lender discussion |
| 30–40 min | Buyer agency agreement review and signing |
| 40–50 min | Buyer presentation — process, search setup, offer strategy |
| 50–55 min | Neighborhood/area discussion; set up BLC search |
| 55–60 min | Next steps: pre-approval if needed, first showing tour date |

**If buyer pushes back on signing agency agreement before seeing homes:**
- "I completely understand — let's do a property-specific agreement for the first home you want to see. That way we're covered for that showing and you can evaluate if this is the right fit."
- IAR Form 125 can be scoped to a single property address to reduce commitment friction for first meeting

## Difficult Conversation Scripts

### Buyer Has Unrealistic Price Expectations
"Let me show you what $[X] gets you in [target area] right now. [Show 3 recent sales at their budget.] These are the homes that have actually sold. If we want more space or a specific location, we need to look at what that's costing — here's what $[Y] opens up."

Key principle: show, don't tell. Market data ends the argument a verbal explanation starts.

### Buyer Won't Get Pre-Approved
"I want to help you find your home. But I can't write a competitive offer without a pre-approval letter. In this market, a seller won't accept an offer without financing documentation. And honestly, I'd be doing you a disservice if we found the perfect home and then couldn't move on it. The pre-approval takes 3–5 business days. Let me introduce you to [lender name] — he/she is great with first calls."

### Buyer Has Previously Bad Agent Experience
"I hear you. What happened before?" [Listen fully.] "Here's how we work differently: [specific differentiators from agent-profile.yaml]. And if at any point this isn't working, I want you to tell me."

Key principle: acknowledge first. Don't defend your industry — differentiate yourself.
