# Offer Terms Guide — Central Indiana / Indiana Purchase Agreement

> **Legal Disclaimer:** This document is an educational reference for licensed Indiana real estate agents. It does not constitute legal advice. Consult your managing broker and/or a licensed Indiana real estate attorney for guidance on specific contract situations.

## Indiana Purchase Agreement (IAR Form 150) — Key Terms Reference

### Earnest Money — Detailed Norms

**By Price Range (Central Indiana, 2025–2026 norms):**

| Purchase Price | Minimum (1%) | Standard (1.5%) | Strong (2–3%) | Very Competitive |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| $150,000–$200,000 | $1,500 | $2,500 | $3,000–$6,000 | $6,000+ |
| $200,000–$300,000 | $2,000 | $3,500 | $4,000–$9,000 | $9,000+ |
| $300,000–$400,000 | $3,000 | $5,000 | $6,000–$12,000 | $12,000+ |
| $400,000–$600,000 | $4,000 | $7,500 | $8,000–$18,000 | $18,000+ |
| $600,000–$900,000 | $6,000 | $10,000 | $12,000–$27,000 | $27,000+ |
| $900,000+ | $9,000 | $15,000+ | Negotiated | Case-by-case |

**Earnest Money Due Dates:**
- Standard: 2–3 business days from acceptance
- Competitive offer: 1 business day strengthens the signal
- Cash deals: 3–5 days is common (buyer wants to verify title first)

**Earnest Money Holder:**
- Options: Buyer's brokerage, Listing brokerage, Title company (specified in agreement)
- Best practice: title company when both agents are different brokerages to avoid disputes
- Funds must be deposited into escrow — not held in agent's personal account (IREC rule)

**Earnest Money Disputes:**
- Indiana: if buyer defaults without legal cause, seller typically keeps earnest money as liquidated damages
- If seller defaults, buyer entitled to full return plus may seek specific performance
- If parties cannot agree on disposition: holding brokerage may interplead with court (slow)
- Fastest resolution: mutual written release signed by both parties

## Contingency Deep Dives

### Inspection Contingency

**Standard Indiana terms:**
- Inspection period: 10 days from acceptance
- Buyer selects own inspector
- After inspection, buyer has 3 options:
  1. Accept the property as-is (waive remaining contingency)
  2. Submit an Inspection Response requesting repairs, credits, or price reduction
  3. Terminate the contract and receive earnest money refund

**Inspection period shortening (competitive market):**
- 7 days: acceptable in hot Hamilton County market with a responsive listing agent
- 5 days: tight but doable if inspector has fast turnaround; flagged as aggressive
- 3 days: very aggressive; only if buyer knows a highly responsive inspector
- 0 days (waived): buyer accepts property in current condition; earnest money at risk for condition issues

**Inspection Response norms:**
- Request repairs for: safety hazards, major systems (roof, HVAC, plumbing, electrical), structural concerns
- Let go: cosmetic issues, normal wear, deferred maintenance under $500
- Credit requests: preferred over repair requests in Indiana practice — seller often poor at repairs
- Typical acceptable ask: $1,500–$8,000 on a $300K–$400K home depending on findings
- Red flags that warrant termination: major foundation cracks, knob-and-tube wiring, extensive mold, failing septic

**Inspector selection:**
- Pull from agent's vendor-network.yaml
- Average Indiana inspection fee: $350–$550 for SFR; sewer scope additional $150–$250; radon test additional $150
- Typical turnaround: report within 24–48 hours
- Agent should NOT recommend the cheapest inspector — independence and quality matter for buyer's protection

**Radon in Indiana:**
Indiana has one of the highest radon rates in the country. Zone 1 (highest risk) covers most of Central Indiana including all of Hamilton, Boone, Hendricks, Marion, and Johnson Counties. A radon test is NOT optional — recommend it on every property. Mitigation cost if elevated: $800–$1,200 typical.

### Financing Contingency

**Standard Indiana terms:**
- Approval deadline: 21 days from acceptance is standard
- Can be shortened: 14 days for fully underwritten pre-approvals in competitive situations
- If lender cannot approve: buyer terminates and receives earnest money back
- If buyer's financing falls through after contingency deadline: earnest money at risk

**When to shorten:**
- Buyer has TBD (to be determined) underwriting approval with loan fully documented
- Buyer has large down payment reducing lender risk
- Lender provides written commitment that closing is 95%+ certain

**When NOT to waive:**
- Buyer is not truly cash — do not waive on behalf of a buyer using financing
- Waiving financing contingency means buyer risks earnest money if loan fails
- Agent should not advise buyer to waive financing contingency without lender written blessing

**Cash verification:**
For cash offers, seller's agent typically asks for Proof of Funds (POF):
- Bank statement, investment account statement, or wire confirmation letter
- Should reflect funds available (not just account balance — confirm accessible)
- Buyer does not need to disclose more than enough to cover purchase price

### Appraisal Contingency

**Standard Indiana terms:**
- Property must appraise at or above purchase price
- If appraisal < purchase price: buyer may terminate or renegotiate
- Lender will not finance above appraised value in most conventional/FHA loans (LTV-based)

**Appraisal Gap Coverage:**
When buyer wants to strengthen an offer in a seller's market:

- Option 1: Waive appraisal contingency entirely (strongest; buyer pays the gap regardless of amount)
- Option 2: Cover gap up to a capped amount (e.g., "Buyer will cover up to $10,000 of any appraisal gap")
- Option 3: Share the gap (buyer covers 50% of gap up to $X)

**Gap calculation for agent:**
```
Appraisal Gap = Purchase Price - Appraised Value
Buyer's Gap Obligation = Min(Gap Coverage Ceiling, Actual Gap)
Cash Needed at Closing = Down Payment + Normal Closing Costs + Gap Obligation
```

**Appraisal challenge process:**
If appraisal comes in low and buyer does NOT have gap coverage:
1. Buyer's agent can request a Reconsideration of Value (ROV) — provide comparable sales the appraiser may have missed
2. Agent cannot provide their own CMA to the appraiser as evidence of value (USPAP restriction)
3. If ROV is unsuccessful: renegotiate purchase price OR buyer walks with earnest money intact (if contingency in place)

**Indiana appraisal timeline:**
- Standard market: 10–14 days from order to report
- VA appraisal: 10–21 days; VA assigns the appraiser (agent/buyer cannot select)
- FHA appraisal: Similar to conventional; appraiser flags health/safety issues for correction
- Appraisal validity: Most conventional appraisals valid for 120 days (can be extended to 240)

### Home Sale Contingency

**Structure:**
- Buyer's purchase is contingent on the successful sale (closing) of their current home
- Seller may accept a "kick-out clause": seller continues marketing; if new acceptable offer received, buyer has X days (typically 48–72 hours) to remove contingency or release

**Why sellers often resist:**
- Adds uncertainty and delay to the transaction
- Seller becomes dependent on a separate transaction they have no control over
- In a seller's market: listing agent can often find non-contingent buyer quickly

**When it's acceptable:**
- Buyer's market: fewer competing offers; seller may accept
- Unique buyer (very high earnest money, perfect offer terms)
- Agent has a buyer who could bridge financially if pressed

**Alternatives to present to the buyer:**
1. List their current home first — accept an offer before writing contingent purchase offer
2. Bridge loan — short-term financing to buy before selling
3. Simultaneous close — coordinate both closings on same day through same title company
4. Leaseback on current home — sell and rent back from buyer for 30–60 days while finding new home

## Offer Strength Assessment Matrix

When advising buyer OR evaluating offers as listing agent, rate each dimension:

| Dimension | Weak | Moderate | Strong |
|---|---|---|---|
| Price vs comps | >3% below comp value | At comp value | Above comp value |
| Earnest money | <1% | 1–2% | >2% |
| Financing type | Unverified / FHA on distressed | FHA on clean / Conventional | Conventional / Cash |
| Pre-approval | Missing or pre-qual only | Full pre-approval | TBD underwrite / All-cash POF |
| Inspection | Standard 10 day | Shortened to 7 day | Pre-inspection / Waived |
| Appraisal | Standard | Gap coverage $X | Waived / Cash |
| Financing contingency | Standard 21 day | Shortened to 14 day | Waived / Cash |
| Closing date | Unclear / Long | 30–45 days | < 30 days or seller-preferred |
| Concessions | Large request | Small request | None |
| Personal letter | Not provided | Provided | N/A |

**Overall score interpretation:**
- Mostly Strong: very competitive; likely wins in multiple-offer
- Mixed: good core but one or two weak dimensions; coach buyer on which to fix
- Mostly Weak: needs coaching before submitting; may not be taken seriously

## Personal Letter Fair Housing Compliance

**Background:**
Personal letters (also called "love letters") can expose sellers to fair housing claims if the letter reveals protected class characteristics and the seller uses that information in their selection. Some listing agents decline to pass letters to their seller clients for this reason.

**Protected classes under federal Fair Housing Act:**
Race, Color, Religion, National Origin, Sex, Familial Status, Disability

**Indiana additional protected class:**
Ancestry

**What triggers risk:**
- Letters mentioning children, family size, or "growing family" → familial status
- Letters referencing church attendance, religious holidays, or beliefs → religion
- Photos of the buyer that reveal race, ethnicity, or national origin
- References to accessibility needs, conditions, or adaptive equipment → disability
- References to the neighborhood's "character," "community," or "people" → potential steering

**Safe letter content checklist:**
- ✅ Specific architectural or design features the buyer loves
- ✅ The buyer's appreciation for the yard, garden, or outdoor space (without child references)
- ✅ General lifestyle statements ("We love cooking" = fine; "we love cooking for our family" = borderline)
- ✅ Connection to the neighborhood without protected class subtext
- ✅ Statement of buyer's commitment to care for the home
- ❌ No photos of the buyers
- ❌ No mention of children, pregnancy, or "growing family"
- ❌ No mention of religion, church, or faith community
- ❌ No mention of national origin, cultural background, or ethnicity

**Agent note:** Before writing a personal letter, confirm the listing agent will pass it along. If listing agent has a no-letter policy, respect it.

## Multiple Offer Management — Listing Agent Protocols

### Setting Up a Highest-and-Best Call

**Timing:**
- Call H&B as soon as 2+ offers are received and verified in writing
- Give buyers at least 4–12 hours to respond (depending on urgency and time of day)
- Avoid very late-night deadlines — they create logistics problems

**What to disclose to buyers' agents:**
- Number of offers: YES — required to be honest
- Price of competing offers: NO — do not reveal competing prices
- Identity of other buyers: NO
- Whether offer is the current leader: NO
- Your seller's bottom line: ABSOLUTELY NOT

**What sellers can do with multiple offers:**
1. Accept one outright (can be done before H&B deadline if one is clearly dominant)
2. Send simultaneous counteroffers to top candidates (must be clearly labeled as counter, not acceptance; use IAR counter form)
3. Reject all and re-list (unusual but occasionally correct if all offers are below expectation)

### Counter Strategy

**Single counter (seller has a clear preferred buyer):**
- Counter only that buyer's offer
- Standard counter form: IAR Counter Proposal (separate from the purchase agreement)

**Multiple simultaneous counters:**
- Seller counters multiple buyers; first acceptance wins
- CRITICAL: counter must clearly state "This counter is being sent to multiple buyers simultaneously. The first executed counter returned to listing agent constitutes acceptance. All other counters become void."
- If not clearly labeled, agent could create multiple simultaneous binding contracts

**Verbal counters:**
- Do NOT advise verbal counters in Indiana real estate — all counter terms should be in writing
- Verbal agreement is not enforceable for real property in Indiana (Statute of Frauds)
