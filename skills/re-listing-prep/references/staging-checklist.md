# Staging Checklist — Central Indiana Listings

Complete reference for property preparation. Use as a seller handout or an
agent walkthrough guide. Every item is actionable, not categorical.

---

## Room-by-Room Checklist

### Exterior and Curb Appeal

- [ ] Lawn mowed and edged (mow within 48 hours of photos; not the morning of
      — grass clippings are messy)
- [ ] Driveway and front walk power-washed
- [ ] Front porch swept and cleaned
- [ ] Cobwebs removed from eaves, porch ceiling, light fixtures
- [ ] Front door cleaned; repaint if scratched, faded, or peeling
- [ ] Door hardware cleaned or replaced (tarnished hardware reads as neglected)
- [ ] House numbers visible and not rusted
- [ ] Welcome mat: new or freshly cleaned (no worn mats)
- [ ] Seasonal flowers or potted plants at entry (1–2 is ideal; more looks cluttered)
- [ ] Mulch freshened in front beds (3-inch layer; bags from Home Depot work fine)
- [ ] Shrubs trimmed (hedges squared, ornamentals shaped)
- [ ] Dead plants or annuals removed from beds
- [ ] Gutters cleaned; no hanging sections
- [ ] Garage door cleaned; if operable but dented/dated, consider replacement
- [ ] Side yards cleared: garden hoses, bikes, yard equipment, kids' toys
- [ ] Trash cans moved to backyard or garage — never visible in photos
- [ ] Vehicles removed from driveway and street in front of home for all photos
- [ ] Backyard: mowed, edged, clutter removed (no kid toys, dog items, unsightly
      storage visible)
- [ ] Deck or patio: styled with furniture (clean chairs and table, no plastic
      covers), umbrella open if weather is good
- [ ] Pool: cover removed, water clean and blue, toys removed

### Entry / Foyer

- [ ] Entry table or console cleared of mail, keys, sunglasses, personal items
- [ ] Coat closet closed or tidy (buyers will open it)
- [ ] Lighting: replace any burned-out bulbs; set to maximum wattage for photos
- [ ] Flooring cleaned (tile scrubbed, hardwood swept and Swiffered)
- [ ] Mirror cleaned if present
- [ ] One tasteful decorative item maximum (small plant, single candle, simple
      vase — not a collection)

### Living Room / Family Room

- [ ] Remove 30–40% of furniture — the room should feel open, not full
- [ ] Remaining furniture arranged conversationally around a focal point
      (fireplace, TV, view window)
- [ ] Remove all family photos from walls and shelves
- [ ] Remove personal collections: trophies, figurines, sports memorabilia
- [ ] Bookshelves: thin out books and objects to 50–60% capacity; style
      remaining items by color or height grouping
- [ ] TV: display a landscape screensaver or turn off for photos; never show a
      dark black screen — it reflects the camera
- [ ] Throws and pillows: fresh, neutral; no worn or pilled fabric
- [ ] Fireplace: clean glass, tidy hearth, logs stacked or removed if messy
- [ ] Overhead and lamp lighting: all on, max wattage, for photos
- [ ] Rugs: clean, or replace if worn; must lie flat (no curled corners)
- [ ] Window coverings: open all blinds and shades to maximum; pull curtains back
      as far as they will go
- [ ] Ceiling fans: clean blades; set to low speed for photos if moving water is
      needed; turn off if they create motion blur

### Kitchen

- [ ] Countertops completely clear — only permitted: one small styled grouping
      (e.g., a wooden cutting board, a small plant or fruit bowl, a coffee
      maker if high-end) — nothing more
- [ ] All appliances wiped clean inside and out (oven, microwave, refrigerator
      exterior)
- [ ] Refrigerator cleared of magnets, photos, kids' art
- [ ] Cabinet fronts wiped (grease buildup is a common miss)
- [ ] Sink scrubbed; no dishes in sink or drying rack
- [ ] Under-sink cabinet tidy (buyers open it)
- [ ] Pantry organized: labels facing forward, canned goods grouped, boxes
      stacked
- [ ] Backsplash cleaned and grout scrubbed
- [ ] Cabinet hardware: clean or replace if tarnished
- [ ] Kitchen table or island: cleared and styled with one simple arrangement
      (place settings, a small plant, or nothing at all)
- [ ] Garbage can: in cabinet or out of frame for photos
- [ ] Vent hood cleaned; filters removed or replaced if visible

### Primary Bedroom

- [ ] Bed freshly made: crisp hotel-style bedding; neutral duvet or comforter
      (white or cream photographs best)
- [ ] Decorative pillows arranged (2–4 maximum; no lumpy or pilling pillows)
- [ ] Nightstands: one lamp each, one book or small item — no medications,
      chargers, glasses, personal effects
- [ ] Dresser: clear top entirely or style with one simple grouping
- [ ] Closet: organized and accessible — remove 30–50% of hanging items, fold
      or store visible floor clutter; buyers will open master closet
- [ ] Master bath visible from bedroom: toilet lid down, countertop cleared
- [ ] Artwork: remove personal family photos; leave neutral art or remove
      entirely
- [ ] Under-bed storage: not visible; use a bed skirt if necessary

### Secondary Bedrooms

- [ ] Single clear purpose per room: bedroom, office, or workout space — not
      a combination with visible storage overflow
- [ ] Beds made; neutral bedding
- [ ] Desks cleared of clutter if used as office
- [ ] Closets: closed or organized; do not stuff

### Bathrooms (all)

- [ ] Toilet lid down for all photos and showings
- [ ] Counter completely clear: no toothbrushes, toothpaste tubes, hair tools,
      personal products, prescriptions
- [ ] One permitted counter display: a candle, a small soap dispenser, or a
      small plant — not all three
- [ ] Towels: freshly washed, neatly folded, or rolled; matching set; neutral
      color preferred (white or gray)
- [ ] Mirror cleaned (streaks are very visible in photos)
- [ ] Shower: glass doors cleaned (no water spots or soap scum); shower
      curtain washed and hanging straight; shampoo/soap products removed
      or hidden
- [ ] Toilet: scrubbed inside bowl; clean exterior
- [ ] Toilet seat: replace if cracked or discolored
- [ ] Grout: re-caulk if discolored or gapping; this is one of the highest-
      impact low-cost improvements in bathrooms
- [ ] Exhaust fan: cleaned of dust buildup (very visible in photos)
- [ ] Lighting: all bulbs on; replace burned-out; consider upgrading wattage

### Basement — Finished

- [ ] Styled as a functional living space, not a storage zone
- [ ] Furniture arranged as it would be for TV/rec/playroom use
- [ ] All storage organized and labeled; if exercise equipment, display it
      purposefully
- [ ] Dehumidifier: running and emptied day before photos; do not leave it in
      the middle of the room
- [ ] Bar area: styled; glasses displayed
- [ ] Egress windows: clean; ensure window wells are leaf-free

### Basement — Unfinished

- [ ] Floor swept; no standing water or visible moisture
- [ ] Storage organized and labeled (organized storage signals a well-maintained
      home; chaotic storage signals neglect)
- [ ] Mechanicals accessible and clean; note ages of water heater, furnace on
      a label affixed to unit (buyers ask; having it ready builds trust)
- [ ] No combustibles stored near mechanicals

### Garage

- [ ] Vehicles removed for all photos
- [ ] Floor swept
- [ ] Wall storage organized: tools hung, bikes mounted, shelving tidy
- [ ] No loose boxes stacked randomly
- [ ] Workbench: organized; not just cleared to one side

## Common Seller Staging Mistakes

These are the mistakes agents see most often. Flag them proactively.

1. **"Clean enough" is not photo-ready clean.** Sellers have nose blindness to
   their own home. Professional deep clean before photos is non-negotiable.

2. **Leaving family photos on every wall and shelf.** Buyers struggle to
   picture themselves in a home surrounded by another family. Photos must go.

3. **Overstuffed closets.** Every buyer opens every closet. A packed closet
   reads as "not enough storage" even if the home has plenty of closet space.

4. **Leaving personal products in bathrooms.** Nothing makes a bathroom feel
   less like a spa retreat than 15 shampoo bottles and a razor in the shower.

5. **Cluttered kitchen countertops.** The kitchen is where listings are won and
   lost. One appliance maximum on any countertop. Everything else goes away.

6. **Dark photos from insufficient lighting.** Sellers close curtains and only
   turn on one lamp. Every light in the house should be on for photos,
   curtains open wide.

7. **Lawn not mowed the day of photos.** Mow 1–2 days before; not the morning
   of (clippings are a mess and photos show tire tracks).

8. **Not removing cars.** Driveway and street should be empty for exterior
   shots. A single car in the driveway blocks the home, shrinks the garage,
   and fills a frame that should show the property.

9. **Leaving garbage cans visible.** Move them to the backyard or interior
   garage before exterior photos.

10. **"We'll just clean up the day of photos."** This never goes well. Deep
    clean, declutter, and stage over 3–5 days minimum. The day before is for
    final touch-up and last-minute items.

## Photographer Prep Checklist — The Day Before Photos

This checklist goes to the seller 48 hours before the photo appointment.

**Exterior:**
- [ ] Lawn mowed and edged
- [ ] Driveway and walk power-washed and dry
- [ ] Front flowers watered and looking fresh
- [ ] All vehicles removed from driveway and curb
- [ ] Trash cans moved out of sight
- [ ] Backyard cleared: no toys, tools, pet items
- [ ] Pool cover off (if pool)

**Interior:**
- [ ] All lights on — every room, every lamp, every fixture
- [ ] All window blinds open as wide as they will go
- [ ] All ceiling fans off (they blur in photos)
- [ ] Toilet seats down in all bathrooms
- [ ] All personal items removed from bathroom counters
- [ ] Kitchen counters completely clear
- [ ] All beds freshly made
- [ ] All family photos removed from walls
- [ ] Pets and pet items (bowls, beds, crates) removed from property
- [ ] Final vacuum/Swiffer of all floors
- [ ] Garage vehicles removed; floor swept

**Day of:**
- [ ] Leave the home during photography — sellers in the home slows the
      photographer and adds stress
- [ ] Leave all lights on as set up
- [ ] Leave thermostat at comfortable temperature
- [ ] Give photographer full access; introduce them to any access codes

## Seasonal Considerations — Central Indiana

**Spring (March–May) — Best season for exterior photos:**
- Indiana spring is peak listing season. Exterior photos in late April and
  May with blooming trees and green lawn significantly outperform winter or
  fall listings on visual appeal.
- Schedule photos after forsythia and redbud trees bloom for color.
- Avoid scheduling after heavy rain — mud and saturated grass show poorly.
- Twilight photos work beautifully in spring with longer days.

**Summer (June–August):**
- Excellent greenery, but heat can make lawn go dormant and golden if
  drought hits. Water lawn during listing prep.
- Pool photos: prioritize mid-morning to show clear water without sun glare.
- HVAC systems show their age in summer. If HVAC is old, consider a home
  warranty rather than replacement.

**Fall (September–November) — Second-best season:**
- Fall color (October peak in Central Indiana) can be a visual selling point.
  Schedule photos during peak foliage.
- Curb appeal deteriorates quickly as leaves drop; plan for additional yard
  cleanup before photos if listing in November.
- Shorter days make afternoon twilight exterior shots easier to schedule.

**Winter (December–February):**
- Challenging for exterior photography: dormant grass, gray skies, short
  days. Options:
  - Schedule photos on a sunny day when snow is absent or fresh and white
    (not gray slush).
  - Some agents use spring/summer exterior photos from a previous season
    if the property has been listed before — check MIBOR rules on this.
  - Twilight photos help in winter; lights glow warmly and make the
    exterior inviting despite bare trees.
  - Interior staging importance increases in winter since exterior is less
    compelling.

## Virtual Staging — Disclosure Requirements per MIBOR Rules

**What virtual staging is:**
Digital addition of furniture and decor to photos of empty rooms.

**What it is NOT:**
- A way to digitally remove existing features (popcorn ceilings, dated
  fixtures, stains) — this is misrepresentation
- A substitute for decluttering an occupied home
- Equivalent to physical staging for high-end listings

**MIBOR disclosure requirement:**
All virtually staged photos submitted to the BLC must be labeled "Virtually
Staged" either:
- Directly on the image (watermark or text overlay), or
- In the BLC photo caption/description field for that photo

Failure to label virtually staged photos is a MIBOR policy violation and may
constitute misrepresentation to buyers.

**Best practices:**
- Use virtual staging only for furnishing empty spaces, not for masking
  property condition issues
- Keep virtual staging consistent with the actual property — do not add
  finishes or features that do not exist
- Label prominently — buyers should never reach a showing unaware that the
  home is vacant
