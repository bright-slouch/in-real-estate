# Closing Checklist — Central Indiana

> Comprehensive reference for `re-closing-coordinator`. All items organized by phase and responsible party.

---

## Phase 1: Clear to Close (CTC) — Day of CTC Through Day Before Closing

### Lender Actions (not agent-controlled, but agent monitors)
- [ ] Closing Disclosure (CD) delivered to buyer — must be at least 3 business days before closing (federal TRID)
- [ ] Loan approval formally issued
- [ ] Homeowners insurance binder received and approved by lender
- [ ] Final Verification of Employment (VOE) completed (often day before or morning of close)
- [ ] Wire instructions sent to title company for loan funding

### Title Company Actions
- [ ] All title exceptions and requirements cleared
- [ ] Settlement statement (HUD/ALTA) prepared
- [ ] Closing packet assembled (deed, mortgage, note, all addenda, affidavits)
- [ ] Lien releases and payoffs confirmed (for seller's mortgage, HOA liens, etc.)
- [ ] Closing appointment scheduled with all parties
- [ ] County recorder's office notified for post-closing recording

### Buyer's Agent Actions
- [ ] Confirm CTC received from lender
- [ ] Confirm CD received by buyer and 3-day window has started
- [ ] Schedule final walkthrough (24–48 hours before close)
- [ ] Confirm buyer has closing funds: amount and method (wire or cashier's check)
- [ ] Verbally confirm wire instructions with title company — provide buyer the title company's DIRECT phone number
- [ ] Confirm buyer has photo ID ready
- [ ] Provide utility transfer list to buyer (see utility section below)
- [ ] Confirm buyer has arranged homeowners insurance (binder needed)
- [ ] Confirm buyer's moving arrangements don't conflict with closing/recording timeline

### Listing Agent Actions
- [ ] Confirm seller has completed all agreed repairs
- [ ] Confirm seller has arranged moving timeline to vacate before final walkthrough
- [ ] Confirm all keys, openers, access codes, fobs are gathered
- [ ] Confirm seller has notified utilities for transfer effective closing date
- [ ] Confirm seller is not canceling homeowners insurance before deed records
- [ ] If post-possession agreement: confirm IAR Form 196 is signed; seller knows move-out deadline

## Phase 2: Final Walkthrough Checklist

> Conduct 24–48 hours before closing. Use this checklist on-site. Document any issues with photos.

### Every Room
- [ ] All light switches and electrical outlets functional
- [ ] All windows open, close, and lock
- [ ] All interior doors open, close, and latch; deadbolts where present
- [ ] Ceilings: no new stains, water damage, or marks since inspection
- [ ] Walls: no new holes, damage from move-out
- [ ] Flooring: no new scratches, chips, stains, or damage

### Kitchen
- [ ] Refrigerator present and cooling (if included in sale)
- [ ] Range/oven functional — test burners and oven heat
- [ ] Microwave functional (if over-range/built-in and included)
- [ ] Dishwasher runs and drains
- [ ] Garbage disposal operational
- [ ] Kitchen faucet — water pressure acceptable, no drips or leaks
- [ ] Under-sink cabinet — no new moisture or evidence of leaks
- [ ] All cabinets and drawers functional

### Bathrooms (each)
- [ ] Toilet flushes; tank fills; no running
- [ ] Shower and tub faucets — hot and cold water, no drips
- [ ] Bathroom sink — functional; no leaks
- [ ] Exhaust fan operational
- [ ] Caulking around tub/shower in same condition as at inspection

### HVAC System
- [ ] Thermostat operational; set to appropriate heating or cooling mode
- [ ] System runs and air comes from all supply registers
- [ ] Return air filter present
- [ ] Furnace/air handler accessible and in same condition as at inspection

### Water Heater
- [ ] Present and in same location as at inspection
- [ ] Pilot light on (gas) or indicator light on (electric)
- [ ] No new rust or leaks around base

### Basement / Crawl Space
- [ ] No new water intrusion, standing water, or flood damage
- [ ] Sump pump present, plugged in, and operational (if applicable)
- [ ] No evidence of new pest intrusion or moisture-related damage

### Garage
- [ ] Garage door opens and closes with remote opener
- [ ] Garage door emergency pull cord present
- [ ] All garage door openers and keypad codes provided
- [ ] No new damage to garage walls, ceiling, or floor from move-out

### Exterior
- [ ] No significant new damage to siding, trim, gutters, or roof visible from ground
- [ ] Driveway and walkways in same condition as at offer
- [ ] Landscaping — no significant damage from move-out (ruts from moving truck, etc.)
- [ ] Fence gates functional (if applicable)
- [ ] Outdoor spigots accessible

### Personal Property and Access Devices
- [ ] All keys present and accounted for (front door, back door, side door, etc.)
- [ ] Mailbox key(s) present
- [ ] HOA gate fob or code provided (if applicable)
- [ ] Pool key or fob (if applicable)
- [ ] Garage door remotes — all units accounted for
- [ ] Security system code or disarm instructions (if applicable)

### Agreed Repairs
- [ ] Each repair item from the inspection agreement has been completed
- [ ] Buyer has had a chance to verify quality of repairs (not just that work was done)
- [ ] Permits pulled for work requiring permits (HVAC replacement, electrical panel, etc.)
- [ ] Receipts or invoices from contractor provided (courteous, not always required)

### Excluded Property
- [ ] All seller's personal property NOT included in the sale has been removed
- [ ] No debris, junk, or unwanted items left behind
- [ ] Staging items removed (if staged — those items belong to the stager, not the buyer)

## Phase 3: Closing Day Checklist

### Buyer
- [ ] Government-issued photo ID — all buyers signing (driver's license or passport)
- [ ] Cashier's check or wire confirmation (per Closing Disclosure amount)
- [ ] Wire instructions verbally confirmed with title company (wire fraud prevention)
- [ ] Checkbook for minor adjustments
- [ ] Social Security number(s) available (for IRS Form 1099-S if applicable)

### Seller
- [ ] Government-issued photo ID — all sellers signing (driver's license or passport)
- [ ] All keys, openers, access codes, fobs (bring in a labeled bag)
- [ ] Appliance manuals and warranty documents (professional courtesy)
- [ ] HOA documents and contacts
- [ ] Bank routing and account number if seller wants proceeds wired

### Both Parties
- [ ] Closing appointment confirmed at title company
- [ ] Any power of attorney documents if a signer is not present in person (requires advance title approval)

### Agent Actions at Closing
- [ ] Attend closing (or confirm TC is present)
- [ ] Review settlement statement with client before signing
- [ ] Confirm all numbers match Closing Disclosure (for buyer) or net sheet estimate (for seller)
- [ ] Confirm agent commission line items are correct
- [ ] After closing: confirm deed has recorded with county (check title company's recording confirmation)
- [ ] Transfer keys to buyer after deed recording confirmed

## Phase 4: Post-Closing (Same Day Through 1 Year)

### Day of Closing
- [ ] Send congratulations email to client (within 2 hours of recording)
- [ ] Add client to CRM as "past client"
- [ ] Update ShowingTime, BLC/MLS to "Sold" status
- [ ] Remove lockbox and for-sale sign (listing side)
- [ ] Document final transaction in agent's records

### Within 48 Hours
- [ ] Order closing gift — see `templates/closing-gift-tracker.md` for ideas by client type and budget
- [ ] Add client to post-closing follow-up schedule

### 7–10 Days Post-Closing
- [ ] Send review request (Google Business Profile + Zillow)
- [ ] Deliver closing gift with handwritten note

### 30 Days Post-Closing
- [ ] 30-day check-in call or email: "How's the new home? Anything I can help with?"
- [ ] Indiana homestead exemption reminder: file with county assessor by December 31 of purchase year
- [ ] Offer to help with any contractor or service provider referrals

### 6 Months Post-Closing
- [ ] Seasonal maintenance tips email
- [ ] Check in on how they're enjoying the home
- [ ] Remind them about homestead exemption if they haven't filed

### 1-Year Anniversary
- [ ] Anniversary card (mailed) or text message
- [ ] Offer a free home value estimate or CMA
- [ ] Remind them of any home improvement projects they mentioned — has value increased?

## Indiana-Specific Closing Notes

### Homestead Exemption
- **What it is:** Property tax reduction for primary residences — reduces assessed value for tax purposes
- **Savings:** Typically $200–$500+ per year depending on county and home value
- **Deadline:** Must be filed with county assessor's office by December 31 of the purchase year
- **How to file:** Online through county assessor's portal or in person at the county assessor's office
- County assessor websites:
  - Marion County: assessor.indy.gov
  - Hamilton County: hamiltoncounty.in.gov/assessor
  - Hendricks County: hendrickscounty.in.gov/assessor
  - Johnson County: johnsoncounty.in.gov/assessor
  - Boone County: boonecounty.in.gov/assessor

### Mortgage Deduction
- Indiana also offers a mortgage deduction on property taxes for primary residences with a mortgage
- Buyer should apply at the same time as the homestead exemption — same form, same office

### Deed Recording
- Deed recorded with county recorder's office (not county clerk)
- Recording typically happens same day as closing; rarely next business day
- Recording confirmation can be obtained from title company or searched on county recorder's website

### Title Insurance (Indiana Practice)
- Indiana practice: seller provides the owner's title insurance policy at closing; cost is a seller closing cost
- Buyer's lender requires a separate lender's title policy (buyer pays this; it only protects the lender)
- Buyer may elect to purchase an enhanced owner's policy for additional coverage — agent should mention this option

### Wire Fraud — Critical Reminder
Real estate closings are targeted by wire fraud criminals who intercept emails and provide fraudulent wire instructions. Indiana buyers lost millions to wire fraud in recent years.

**Protocol every agent must follow:**
1. Never send wire instructions in an email without a verbal confirmation warning
2. Instruct buyer to call the title company directly (using a phone number independently obtained — not from the email with wire instructions) to confirm the wire details
3. Wire funds should ONLY be sent to an account the buyer has verbally confirmed
4. Fraudulent wires cannot be recovered — this is not an insurance situation

## Mail-Away (Remote) Closings

For buyers or sellers closing remotely (out of state, traveling):

1. Contact title company at least 10 days before closing to arrange
2. Title sends the signing package via FedEx or DocuSign (if e-notary is available in Indiana)
3. All signatures requiring notarization must be notarized by an Indiana-licensed notary OR use RON (Remote Online Notarization) — Indiana enacted RON legislation in 2019
4. Signed package must be received by title before the scheduled close date
5. Proceeds wire instructions remain the same — verbal confirmation still required

## Post-Possession Agreement (Seller Stays After Close)

When seller needs time after closing to move:

- **Form:** IAR Form 196 — Temporary Occupancy Agreement
- **Must be signed before closing** — never allow seller to stay without a written agreement
- **Key terms to include:**
  - Daily rent (typically 1/30 of PITI payment or per-diem of mortgage interest)
  - Security deposit (typically $500–$2,000)
  - Hard move-out deadline (typically 30–60 days max)
  - Consequences of holdover
  - Who pays utilities during the possession period
- **Risk note:** Buyer's homeowners insurance may not cover the property during seller's post-possession (buyer's insurer considers seller a "tenant"). Buyer should notify their insurance company immediately.
- **Maximum term:** Lenders typically allow 60-day post-possession; longer terms may require lender approval

## New Construction Closing Notes

New construction closings have additional items:

- [ ] Builder's warranty documentation (typically 1-year workmanship + 2-year mechanical + 10-year structural)
- [ ] Certificate of Occupancy (CO) issued by municipality — required before buyer can occupy
- [ ] Final inspection by buyer (not a home inspection — a builder walkthrough with a punch list)
- [ ] Punch list items documented in writing and a completion date agreed
- [ ] Survey of the lot (builder typically provides)
- [ ] HOA documents for new developments (Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions)
- [ ] Builder's preferred lender may offer credits — buyer should compare with outside lender
- [ ] Property taxes for first year may be based on land only (house not on tax rolls yet); expect a reassessment in year 2
