# Transaction Timeline Template

> Standard 30-45 day timeline from accepted offer to closing for a financed Indiana residential transaction. Actual timelines vary based on loan type, inspection findings, title issues, and lender processing speed.

---

## Overview

| Milestone | Day Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Offer accepted | Day 0 | |
| Earnest money due | Days 1-3 | Per purchase agreement terms |
| Home inspection | Days 1-10 | Inspection period (10 days standard) |
| Inspection response | Days 10-12 | Buyer submits repair requests |
| Inspection resolution | Days 12-15 | Seller responds; parties agree |
| Appraisal ordered | Days 7-14 | Lender orders after inspection contingency releases |
| Appraisal completed | Days 14-21 | Typically 7-10 business days after order |
| Loan commitment | Days 21-30 | Lender issues formal commitment letter |
| Title search completed | Days 14-25 | Title company researches ownership history |
| Title commitment issued | Days 20-28 | Title company provides commitment with exceptions |
| Final walkthrough | Day 38-44 | Typically 24-48 hours before closing |
| Closing day | Day 40-45 | Or as specified in purchase agreement |

---

## Day-by-Day Timeline Detail

### Day 0: Offer Accepted

**Agent actions:**
- Distribute fully executed purchase agreement to all parties (buyer, seller, both agents, title company, lender)
- Confirm transaction coordinator is notified (if using TC)
- Create transaction file/folder
- Confirm showing instructions have been updated in ShowingTime (if listing agent)
- Send congratulations communication to client (use email template)

**Buyer agent actions:**
- Submit accepted agreement to buyer's lender immediately
- Remind buyer of earnest money deadline and how to submit payment
- Remind buyer of inspection period deadline
- Schedule home inspector (or confirm buyer will schedule)

---

### Days 1-3: Earnest Money Deposit

**Deadline:** Per purchase agreement — typically 2-3 business days from acceptance

**Buyer actions:**
- Submit earnest money check or wire transfer to holding brokerage or title company
- Confirm receipt with brokerage/title

**Agent confirmation:**
- Verify receipt of earnest money within the agreed timeline
- If buyer fails to deliver earnest money on time, notify all parties immediately — this can be a default condition

---

### Days 1-10: Inspection Period

**Standard:** 10 calendar days from acceptance; 7 days in competitive-market agreements

**Buyer actions:**
- Schedule home inspection within first 2-3 days (inspectors book quickly in busy season)
- Schedule radon test simultaneously with home inspection (most inspectors offer this)
- Consider: sewer scope (recommended for homes 20+ years old or unknown sewer history), WDI (termite/wood-destroying insect), pool inspection, HVAC tune-up/inspection, chimney inspection
- Attend inspection — this is important for buyers to understand the home's condition firsthand

**Agent (buyer's side) actions:**
- Coordinate access with listing agent for inspection
- Attend inspection or review report immediately upon receipt
- Note: Indiana inspection period is a **calendar day deadline** — Day 10 means by end of that day, even if it falls on a weekend

**What happens if nothing is submitted by the inspection deadline?**
Contingency is automatically waived — buyer accepts the property as-is. Agents must calendar this deadline and ensure action happens before it expires.

---

### Days 10-14: Inspection Response

**Buyer submits IAR Form 162 (Inspection Response):**
- List specific repair requests (itemize — not "fix everything")
- Or request closing credit in lieu of repairs
- Or notify seller of withdrawal and request earnest money refund
- Or submit "Notice of Acceptance" removing contingency without requests

**Agent guidance on inspection requests:**
- Prioritize safety, mechanical, and structural items over cosmetic issues
- Lenders may require repairs for FHA/VA loans (peeling paint, missing handrails, etc.)
- A lump-sum credit is often cleaner than requiring specific repairs
- Reasonable requests are more likely to be accepted; aggressive requests can derail deals

---

### Days 12-15: Inspection Response — Seller's Reply (IAR Form 163)

**Seller has options:**
1. Accept all requests
2. Reject all requests
3. Offer alternative (partial credit, different repairs, different credit amount)

**If seller rejects:** Buyer must decide within the original inspection contingency period whether to accept the property as-is or withdraw. If the inspection period has expired, buyer may have limited options.

**Negotiation tips:**
- Keep negotiations professional and data-driven
- Focus on what the market would expect — not personal preferences
- When both sides are close, a split-the-difference approach often resolves impasses

---

### Days 7-21: Appraisal

**Ordered by:** Buyer's lender (not buyer or agent directly)

**When ordered:** After inspection contingency is released (some lenders order simultaneously to save time)

**Timeline:**
- Appraiser scheduled: typically 5-10 days after order in normal market
- Appraisal inspection conducted: 30-60 minutes on-site
- Report delivered to lender: 3-7 business days after inspection
- Lender reviews and sends to buyer: within 1-2 days of receipt

**If appraisal comes in at value:** Move forward
**If appraisal comes in below contract price:** See `indiana-purchase-agreement-guide.md` for options

**Listing agent's role:**
- Provide appraiser with comparable sales data (prepared comp package)
- Ensure property is accessible at scheduled time
- Note any improvements or premium features the appraiser should consider

---

### Days 14-25: Title Search

**Title company actions:**
- Searches county records for ownership history, liens, encumbrances
- Verifies legal description and boundaries
- Checks for outstanding mortgages, judgments, tax liens, IRS liens
- Reviews easements and deed restrictions

**Common title issues in Central Indiana:**
- Outstanding HOA liens (especially on foreclosures or estates)
- Unreleased mortgages from prior refinancing
- Boundary line disputes in older platted neighborhoods
- Estate probate not yet completed
- IRS or state tax liens against seller

**Agent actions if title issue discovered:**
- Work with title company to understand the issue
- Notify all parties immediately
- Assess whether the issue can be resolved before closing
- Extend the close date if needed (use IAR Form 157 — Extension)

---

### Days 21-30: Loan Commitment

**Lender issues commitment letter** after:
- Appraisal completed and at value (or gap resolved)
- Underwriting conditions cleared
- All buyer documentation submitted

**Common underwriting conditions (must be resolved):**
- Updated pay stubs or bank statements
- Explanation letters for large deposits
- Homeowners insurance binder
- HOA contact information for condo loans
- Gift letter documentation for down payment gifts

**Agent actions:**
- Follow up with buyer's lender at Day 20 if commitment not yet issued
- Remind buyer to respond immediately to any lender document requests

---

### Days 20-28: Title Commitment Issued

**Title company issues commitment showing:**
- Exceptions (easements, restrictions) that will appear on the title policy
- Requirements to clear title before closing (liens to be paid, releases needed)

**Buyer review:** Buyer should review title commitment with their attorney or with agent guidance. Most exceptions are standard (utility easements, subdivision restrictions) — but buyers should understand what they're accepting.

---

### Days 28-35: Clear to Close

**"Clear to Close" (CTC)** means the lender has fully approved the loan and is prepared to fund.

**Closing Disclosure (CD) issued:**
- Lender must provide CD to buyer at least **3 business days** before closing
- Buyer should review every line carefully — compare to Loan Estimate issued at application
- Agent should help buyer understand any changes from original estimates

**Final walkthrough scheduled:**
- Typically 24-48 hours before closing
- Confirm property is in agreed-upon condition
- All included personal property still present
- Any agreed repairs completed

---

### Days 38-44: Final Walkthrough

**Purpose:**
- Verify property is in substantially the same condition as at time of offer
- Confirm agreed repairs are completed
- Confirm all included personal property remains (appliances, fixtures agreed upon)
- Verify property is clean and vacant (if that was agreed)

**What to check:**
- Run every faucet, flush every toilet
- Test all appliances included in the sale
- Check for new damage not present at inspection (water intrusion, moving damage)
- Confirm all garage door openers/keys/access devices are present
- Test HVAC — confirm it's functioning seasonally

**If issues found at walkthrough:**
- Minor issues: negotiate a holdback at closing (seller funds held in escrow pending repair)
- Major issues: delay closing until resolved
- Do NOT close if major undisclosed damage is discovered without resolving it first

---

### Day 40-45: Closing Day

**Location:** Title company (seller and buyer may close simultaneously or separately)

**What happens:**
1. Buyer and seller sign all closing documents (deed, mortgage, settlement statement)
2. Lender funds the loan (wire to title company)
3. Buyer provides closing funds (wire or cashier's check per CD)
4. Title company records the deed with county recorder's office
5. Keys are exchanged (usually by agent at property, or via lockbox code)
6. Seller receives their proceeds (wire or check, per their preference)

**What to bring (buyer):**
- Government-issued photo ID (driver's license, passport)
- Proof of wired funds or cashier's check per Closing Disclosure
- Personal checkbook for small adjustments
- Homeowners insurance binder/policy number

**What to bring (seller):**
- Government-issued photo ID
- All keys, garage door openers, access codes, gate codes
- Appliance manuals and warranty documents (professional courtesy)
- HOA documents and contacts (if applicable)

## Common Delays and How to Handle Them

### Appraisal Delay

**Cause:** Appraiser backlog, complex property, rural location, AMC scheduling issues

**Resolution:**
- Contact lender immediately; ask for status
- Request a different appraiser if available (lender controls this)
- Negotiate extension with other party using IAR Form 157
- Most lenders can expedite if asked; escalate to loan officer's manager if needed

### Low Appraisal

**Cause:** Property priced above market support

**Resolution options:**
1. Renegotiate purchase price to appraised value
2. Buyer pays gap out of pocket
3. Buyer challenges appraisal (Reconsideration of Value — ROV)
4. Terminate if appraisal contingency is intact

### Lender Processing Delay

**Cause:** Underwriter backlog, missing documentation, new condition discovered

**Resolution:**
- Identify specific condition causing delay
- Help buyer respond to document requests same day
- Request realistic timeline from lender
- Negotiate extension with other party

### Title Issue

**Cause:** Outstanding lien, unreleased mortgage, boundary dispute, probate issue

**Resolution:**
- Work with title company to obtain releases, clearances, or corrections
- Title company's underwriter may issue over the exception in some cases
- Coordinate with all parties on realistic timeline

### Seller Not Ready to Vacate

**Cause:** Seller's purchase fell through, moving issues, timeline problems

**Resolution:**
- Negotiate a post-closing occupancy agreement (IAR Form 196)
- Ensure agreement is signed before closing
- Include daily rent, security deposit, and hard move-out date
- Never allow seller to stay without a signed occupancy agreement

### Survey Issue (Less Common)

**Cause:** Encroachments, boundary disputes on older properties

**Resolution:** Varies greatly — may require attorney involvement, neighbor negotiation, or title insurance endorsement.

## FHA/VA Specific Timeline Notes

FHA and VA loans typically require **45-50 days** from acceptance to close:
- Additional underwriting requirements and government investor review
- FHA mandatory inspection for property condition (appraiser flags issues)
- VA requires the Certificate of Eligibility, VA appraisal (ordered through VA system — longer lead time)
- Appraisal report review by VA or FHA takes additional time after appraisal completion

Advise buyer clients using FHA/VA to request 45-50 day close dates in their offers. A listing agent who receives an FHA/VA offer with a 30-day close should negotiate a more realistic timeline.
