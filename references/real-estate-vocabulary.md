# Real Estate Vocabulary Reference

> This reference standardizes terminology used across all plugin skills. It includes approved language, terms to avoid for fair housing compliance, Indiana-specific terminology, and common abbreviations.

---

## Fair Housing Language Compliance

### HUD Guidelines Overview

The Fair Housing Act prohibits discrimination in housing based on **race, color, national origin, religion, sex, disability, and familial status**. In advertising and communication, agents must avoid language that:
- Expresses preferences or limitations based on protected classes
- Steers buyers toward or away from neighborhoods based on protected characteristics
- Describes neighborhoods using demographic terms

### Words and Phrases to AVOID

**Demographic/neighborhood steering:**
- ~~"Great neighborhood for families"~~ → "Near top-rated schools and parks"
- ~~"Perfect for couples"~~ → "Low-maintenance living" or "Efficient floor plan"
- ~~"Ideal for empty nesters"~~ → "Single-level living" or "Low upkeep"
- ~~"Mostly [nationality] neighborhood"~~ → Never describe neighborhood demographics
- ~~"Walking distance to [religious institution]"~~ → Never reference as a selling point
- ~~"Good schools"~~ ✓ Generally acceptable (school quality is objective, not demographic)
- ~~"Safe neighborhood"~~ → Avoid — implies other areas are not safe; can be steering
- ~~"Up-and-coming area"~~ → Use cautiously; may imply demographic change

**Disability-related:**
- ~~"Perfect for someone in a wheelchair"~~ → "Wheelchair accessible" or "ADA compliant features" (only if describing actual features)
- ~~"Handicap accessible"~~ → "Accessible" or "ADA-compliant" (not "handicap")
- ~~"Walk-in shower (no steps)"~~ ✓ Acceptable — describes a physical feature
- Never describe a property as unsuitable for people with disabilities

**Familial status:**
- ~~"No children allowed"~~ → Only permitted in qualifying 55+ communities
- ~~"Adults only"~~ → Same — only in qualifying senior housing
- ~~"Quiet building"~~ → Generally acceptable (describes character, not restriction)

### Preferred Alternatives

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|---|---|
| Safe neighborhood | Established neighborhood; low crime statistics available |
| Great for families | Near schools, parks, and recreation |
| Perfect for retirees | Single-level living; low maintenance |
| Exclusive neighborhood | Private community; gated community (if accurate) |
| Master bedroom | Primary bedroom (industry standard has shifted) |
| Master bathroom | Primary bathroom |
| Man cave | Bonus room; recreation room; finished lower level |
| Cozy | Efficient; intimate; charming |
| Motivated seller | Priced to sell; competitive pricing |

## Indiana-Specific Terminology

### BLC vs. MLS

- **BLC (Building and Listing Corporation):** Indiana's MLS platform operated by MIBOR. The correct term in Indiana is "BLC." The term "MLS" is generic and widely understood, but "BLC" is the Indiana-specific term.
- **Active listing:** Currently available for sale in BLC
- **Pending:** Under contract; no longer accepting offers (standard)
- **Contingent:** Under contract but accepting backup offers (when applicable)
- **Sold:** Transaction completed and closed

### Township Assessor vs. County Assessor

Indiana uses a dual assessment system:
- **Township Assessor:** Handles property assessments in most Indiana townships; conducts annual reassessments
- **County Assessor:** Coordinates across townships; handles appeals; issues the official county assessment roll
- **PTABOA (Property Tax Assessment Board of Appeals):** Where assessment appeals go

**Why this matters:** Property tax rates and assessed values vary significantly by township within the same county. A property "in Carmel" may be assessed by Clay Township assessor, and its tax rate reflects both county and township levies.

### Homestead Deduction

Indiana's primary property tax relief:
- **Standard Homestead Deduction:** Reduces assessed value by 60% (up to $45,000 reduction) for owner-occupied primary residences
- **Supplemental Homestead Deduction:** Additional percentage reduction based on assessed value
- **Mortgage Deduction:** Additional deduction if property is mortgaged
- **Effect:** A homestead-eligible property pays significantly lower taxes than a non-homestead (rental/second home) property. Buyers need to verify if the current owner has homestead deduction applied.

**Agent note:** When buyers ask about property taxes, always note whether the amount shown reflects the homestead deduction. If the seller has homestead but the buyer will too, taxes will be similar. If the seller doesn't have homestead (rental) and buyer will (primary residence), taxes may be significantly lower than listed.

### TIF Districts

**Tax Increment Financing (TIF) districts** divert incremental property tax revenue to a development authority for a defined geographic area and time period. Present in many Central Indiana redevelopment areas.

- TIF districts can affect how tax dollars are allocated but don't change the buyer's tax obligation directly
- Agents should disclose if a property is in a TIF district if asked — buyers can verify through county/city records

## Standard Real Estate Terminology

### Transaction Process Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Earnest money | Good-faith deposit held in escrow; demonstrates buyer's intent |
| Under contract | Signed purchase agreement; contingencies may remain |
| Due diligence | Buyer's investigation period (inspection, appraisal, title) |
| Contingency | Condition that must be met for the contract to proceed |
| Kickout clause | Seller's right to accept another offer while buyer contingency is active |
| Bridge loan | Short-term loan allowing buyer to purchase before their current home sells |
| Escalation clause | Offer provision automatically increasing price to beat competing offers |
| Clear to close (CTC) | Lender confirmation that loan is approved and ready to fund |
| HUD-1 / Closing Disclosure | Final settlement statement showing all credits and debits |
| Proration | Dividing ongoing costs (taxes, HOA dues) between buyer and seller at close |

### Property Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| Absorption rate | Rate at which available homes are sold; months of inventory = listings ÷ monthly sales |
| DOM (Days on Market) | Calendar days from active list date to accepted offer date |
| CDOM (Cumulative DOM) | Total days including any prior listing periods for same property |
| List-to-sale ratio | Sale price ÷ list price (100% = sold at list; >100% = above list) |
| Comparable (comp) | Recently sold property used to estimate value of subject property |
| GLA (Gross Living Area) | Finished square footage above grade; excludes basement regardless of finish |
| Non-GLA | Finished basement square footage — included in MIBOR data but often valued differently |
| HOA | Homeowners Association — governing body for subdivisions/condos; collects dues |
| Easement | Right for another party to use a portion of the property (utility, drainage, access) |
| Encumbrance | Any claim or lien against the property |
| Deed restriction | Limitation on property use recorded in the deed |

### Financing Terms

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| LTV (Loan-to-Value) | Loan amount ÷ appraised value; affects PMI requirement |
| PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) | Required when conventional loan LTV > 80% |
| MIP (Mortgage Insurance Premium) | FHA equivalent of PMI |
| Points | Prepaid interest to buy down the interest rate; 1 point = 1% of loan amount |
| APR (Annual Percentage Rate) | Total cost of borrowing including rate, points, and fees |
| Pre-qualification | Informal assessment of buying power; not verified |
| Pre-approval | Verified assessment; application submitted; credit pulled |
| DU/LP Approval | Desktop Underwriter (Fannie Mae) or Loan Prospector (Freddie Mac) automated approval |
| Jumbo loan | Loan exceeding conforming loan limits ($766,550 in 2024 for most Indiana counties) |
| DTI (Debt-to-Income) | Monthly debt ÷ gross monthly income; lenders typically allow up to 43-50% |

## Tone and Language Guidelines for Client Communications

### Consistent Terminology to Use

- **Home** (not "property" or "unit" when referring to someone's primary residence — "property" for investment contexts is fine)
- **Primary bedroom** (not "master bedroom" — industry standard has shifted)
- **Bathroom** or "full bath/half bath" (not "commode")
- **Purchase agreement** (not "contract of sale" — Indiana convention)
- **Lender** or "mortgage company" (not "bank" — not all lenders are banks)
- **Title company" or "closing company" (not "escrow" — Indiana doesn't use escrow language for real estate closings)
- **Closing** (not "settlement" — Indiana convention)

### Phrases That Build Confidence

- "Based on the comparable sales..."
- "The data shows..."
- "In my experience in this market..."
- "Let me verify that and get back to you..."
- "I want to make sure you have all the information before deciding..."

### Phrases to Avoid in Client Communication

- "This is the best deal you'll find" — You don't know that
- "The market is always going up" — Never predict market direction
- "Don't worry about the inspection — that's normal" — Never minimize buyer's right to full inspection
- "The seller will never accept that" — Don't negotiate against your own client
- "I've seen worse" — Unhelpful and minimizing
- "You should make an offer immediately" — Pressure can backfire; present facts, let client decide
