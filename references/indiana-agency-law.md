# Indiana Agency Law Reference

> **Legal Disclaimer:** This document is an educational summary for licensed Indiana real estate agents. It does not constitute legal advice. Consult your managing broker and/or a licensed Indiana real estate attorney for guidance on specific situations. Always comply with Indiana Real Estate Commission (IREC) rules and your brokerage's policies.

---

## Governing Statutes

Indiana agency relationships in real estate are primarily governed by:

- **IC 25-34.1-9-11** — General duties of licensees to buyers and sellers
- **IC 25-34.1-10** — Licensee representation and agency relationships
- **876 IAC 1-1-1 et seq.** — Indiana Real Estate Commission administrative rules
- **NAR Code of Ethics** — Applies to REALTOR® members (MIBOR is NAR member board)

---

## Agency Relationship Types

### 1. Seller's Agent (Listing Agent)

The agent represents the seller exclusively. The agent owes the seller all fiduciary duties (see below). The agent has limited duties to the buyer (unrepresented buyer) — primarily honesty and disclosure of material facts.

**Created by:** Signed listing agreement (IAR Form 103 or equivalent)

**Disclosure requirement:** Agent must disclose to all parties at first substantive contact that they represent the seller.

**Key obligations to seller:**
- Loyalty — act solely in the seller's best interest
- Confidentiality — protect seller's motivation, bottom line, timing
- Disclosure — share all relevant information known to agent
- Obedience — follow lawful instructions
- Reasonable care and diligence

**Obligations to buyer (non-client customer):**
- Honest dealing
- Disclosure of known material defects affecting value
- Cannot make false statements about the property

---

### 2. Buyer's Agent (Buyer Representative)

The agent represents the buyer exclusively. Owes all fiduciary duties to the buyer.

**Created by:** Written Buyer Agency Agreement (IAR Form 125 or equivalent)

**Post-August 2024 requirement:** Per NAR settlement terms, a written buyer agency agreement must be signed before the agent shows any property. See `buyer-agreement-requirements.md` for full details.

**Key obligations to buyer:**
- Loyalty — act solely in the buyer's best interest
- Confidentiality — protect buyer's motivation, financial capacity, timeline
- Disclosure — share all known information that could affect buying decision
- Obedience — follow lawful instructions
- Reasonable care and diligence

**Obligations to seller (non-client):**
- Honest dealing
- Cannot make false statements or misrepresentations

---

### 3. Dual Agent (Limited Agent)

One agent represents BOTH the buyer and seller in the same transaction. This is permitted in Indiana but requires **written informed consent from both parties** before the dual agency arises.

**Governed by:** IC 25-34.1-10-9 — Written informed consent required. Must be obtained BEFORE any confidential information is shared.

**Limitations of dual agency:**
- Cannot advocate for either party's price position
- Cannot share one party's negotiating bottom line with the other
- Must treat both parties equally
- Reduced fiduciary duties to both parties — primarily: honesty, disclosure of material facts, accounting, reasonable care

**When dual agency arises:**
- An agent's own buyer wants to buy one of the agent's own listings
- Both buyer and seller are clients of the same brokerage (company dual agency — see Designated Agency below)

**Required disclosure language (must be signed):**
> "I understand that [Agent/Brokerage] is acting as a limited agent in this transaction, representing both the buyer and the seller. I understand that as a limited agent, [Agent/Brokerage] cannot advocate for my position, cannot reveal confidential information about my motivation or financial position to the other party, and cannot provide me with undivided loyalty. I consent to this limited agency arrangement."

---

### 4. Designated Agency (Recommended Alternative to Dual Agency)

When a brokerage represents both buyer and seller, the managing broker designates different licensees within the brokerage to each represent their respective client. This allows each client to have an advocate while avoiding true dual agency.

**Benefit:** Each designated agent can advocate fully for their own client, unlike in dual agency.

**Requirement:** Both clients must consent in writing to designated agency.

**Available in Indiana:** Yes — most Indiana brokerages use designated agency as the default company policy when both parties are represented within the firm.

---

### 5. Transaction Broker (Facilitator)

Not technically an "agent" in the fiduciary sense. The licensee assists both parties without representing either. Owes duties of honesty, fair dealing, and disclosure of material facts — but NOT fiduciary duties.

**Use cases:**
- Parties who want assistance completing a transaction without full representation
- For-sale-by-owner situations where agent assists with paperwork only
- Less common in Central Indiana residential transactions

**Indiana rule:** Must disclose to both parties that the licensee is acting as a transaction broker, not as an agent representing either party.

## Mandatory Disclosure Requirements

### Agency Disclosure

Indiana requires agents to disclose their agency relationship at **"first substantive contact."**

**What is first substantive contact?**
- First meeting to discuss a specific property
- First showing of a property
- Any conversation where property-specific price, terms, or motivation is discussed
- Does NOT include: open house attendance, general market conversations, property tours without negotiation discussion

**How to disclose:**
1. Verbally state who you represent
2. Provide the **Indiana Agency Disclosure Form** (IAR Form 108 or brokerage equivalent)
3. Have the party sign acknowledging receipt

**Practical script for listing agent meeting unrepresented buyer:**
> "Before we get started, I want to make sure you understand that I represent the seller in this transaction. My job is to get the best price and terms for my client, the seller. I'm happy to answer your questions honestly and share information about the property, but I can't represent your interests in this transaction. You have the right to hire your own buyer's agent who would represent only you."

## Explaining Agency to Clients

### For Seller Clients

**Explain:** "I am your agent. I work exclusively for you. My job is to get you the best possible price and terms. I will keep your motivations, your bottom line, and your timeline confidential from buyers and their agents. I am required to tell buyers about known material defects in the property, but I am not required to share anything that could undermine your negotiating position."

### For Buyer Clients

**Explain:** "I am your buyer's agent. I work exclusively for you. My job is to help you find the right home and protect your interests throughout the transaction. I will keep your budget, your motivation, and your timeline confidential from sellers and listing agents. I'll share everything I know that could affect your decision to buy."

### Common Client Questions

**"Do I have to sign the buyer agreement before I can see homes?"**
Yes, as of August 2024, a written buyer agency agreement is required before showing any property. This is a professional services agreement — it describes what I do for you and how I'm compensated. Most are non-exclusive until you're serious about working together exclusively.

**"What if my agent's brokerage also represents the seller?"**
Your brokerage will designate a different agent to represent the seller, so each of you has your own advocate. This is designated agency and it protects your ability to have full representation.

**"Can you tell me the sellers' bottom line?"**
No — just as a buyer's agent cannot tell me your maximum budget, a listing agent cannot tell me the seller's minimum price. What I can tell you is what the market data supports as fair value.

## Key Case Law and Rule References

- **IREC v. Carrico (2011)** — Established that verbal disclosure alone is insufficient; written disclosure form is required.
- **876 IAC 1-1-2** — Defines "substantive contact" for disclosure timing purposes.
- **876 IAC 1-1-7** — Continuing education requirements related to agency (must complete agency module in each renewal period).

## Summary: Duties Owed by Agency Type

| Duty | Seller's Agent (to seller) | Buyer's Agent (to buyer) | Dual Agent (limited) | Transaction Broker |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Loyalty | Full | Full | Limited | None |
| Confidentiality | Full | Full | Limited | None |
| Disclosure | Full | Full | Material facts only | Material facts only |
| Obedience | Full | Full | Limited | None |
| Reasonable care | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Honesty | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Accounting | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
