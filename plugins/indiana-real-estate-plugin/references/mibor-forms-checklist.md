# MIBOR/IAR Forms Checklist Reference

> **Note:** Form numbers and names are updated periodically by IAR and MIBOR. Always use the current version of each form available through your brokerage's forms platform (Dotloop, ZipForms, or IAR forms library). This checklist reflects standard forms as of early 2026.

---

## Standard IAR Forms for Residential Transactions

### Listing-Side Forms

| Form | Name | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **IAR 103** | Exclusive Right to Sell Listing Agreement | When seller retains agent as exclusive listing broker; most common listing form |
| **IAR 104** | Exclusive Agency Listing Agreement | Seller retains right to sell without commission if buyer is found independently; less common |
| **IAR 108** | Seller's Agency Disclosure | Given to buyers at first substantive contact by listing agent; documents that agent represents seller |
| **Form 46234** | Indiana Seller's Residential Real Estate Sales Disclosure | Must be completed by seller for all applicable residential transfers; given to buyer before or with offer |
| **Form 46232** | Lead Paint Disclosure and Acknowledgment | Required for all homes built before 1978; must be signed by all parties |
| **IAR 200** | Wire Fraud Warning (Seller/Listing) | Provides wire fraud/cybercrime warning to seller; recommended best practice |

---

### Buyer-Side Forms

| Form | Name | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **IAR 125** | Exclusive Buyer Agency Agreement | Signed by buyer before agent shows property (required post-August 2024); establishes representation and compensation terms |
| **IAR 126** | Non-Exclusive Buyer Representation Agreement | Alternative to exclusive — allows buyer to work with multiple agents; less common |
| **IAR 108B** | Buyer's Agency Disclosure | Confirms buyer understands agent represents buyer's interests; signed at first substantive contact |
| **IAR 201** | Wire Fraud Warning (Buyer/Borrower) | Wire fraud/cybercrime warning for buyer; recommended |

---

### Purchase Agreement and Addenda

| Form | Name | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **IAR 150** | Purchase Agreement | The primary contract for all residential sales; establishes all terms |
| **IAR 155** | Counteroffer | When seller wants to propose different terms than buyer's offer; keeps original offer alive |
| **IAR 156** | Amendment | Used to change terms of an already-accepted purchase agreement (price, dates, inclusions, etc.) |
| **IAR 157** | Extension | Used to extend a deadline in an accepted agreement (close date, inspection period, etc.) |
| **IAR 160** | Addendum (General) | Used to add terms not covered in the standard Purchase Agreement |

---

### Contingency-Related Forms

| Form | Name | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **IAR 161** | Home Inspection Contingency Addendum | When parties use a home inspection contingency (typically 10 days in Indiana) |
| **IAR 162** | Inspection Response | Buyer's request for repairs or credits after home inspection; seller then responds |
| **IAR 163** | Inspection Response — Seller's Reply | Seller's acceptance, rejection, or counter to buyer's inspection request |
| **IAR 165** | Financing Contingency Addendum | Documents loan type, rate parameters, and commitment deadline |
| **IAR 166** | Appraisal Contingency Addendum | Protects buyer if home appraises below purchase price |
| **IAR 168** | Home Sale Contingency Addendum | When buyer's offer is contingent on sale of their current home; defines kickout clause rights |
| **IAR 169** | Kickout Notice | When seller activates kickout clause; buyer has defined period to remove home sale contingency or release |
| **IAR 170** | Contingency Removal | When buyer removes a contingency from the agreement (e.g., home sale contingency no longer needed) |

---

### Property-Specific Addenda

| Form | Name | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **IAR 175** | New Construction Addendum | Used instead of or alongside Purchase Agreement for new build transactions; builder may substitute their own form |
| **IAR 176** | FHA/VA Addendum | Required for FHA and VA-financed transactions; includes additional terms and disclosures required by those loan programs |
| **IAR 177** | Condominium/Townhome Addendum | Addresses HOA docs, common elements, right to review condo docs, and related matters |
| **IAR 178** | "As-Is" Sale Addendum | When buyer agrees to purchase in as-is condition; note: seller still must disclose known defects |
| **IAR 179** | Short Sale Addendum | When purchase is contingent on lender approval of a short sale; outlines timeline expectations |

---

### Agent Representation and Disclosure Forms

| Form | Name | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **IAR 108C** | Limited Agency Disclosure | Signed by both parties when agent represents both buyer and seller in the same transaction |
| **IAR 180** | Agent's Disclosure of Interest | Required when agent has personal interest in the transaction (buying a listing, selling own home through own brokerage) |

---

### Closing-Related Forms

| Form | Name | When Used |
|---|---|---|
| **IAR 195** | Possession Agreement | When possession transfers at a time other than closing (early possession or post-close possession by seller) |
| **IAR 196** | Possession Agreement — After Closing | Seller remains in property after closing for defined period; functions like a short-term lease |
| **IAR 198** | Settlement Statement Review Request | Documents any requests to review HUD/CD before closing |

## MIBOR/BLC-Specific Considerations

### BLC Listing Requirements

The BLC (Indiana's MLS operated by MIBOR) has specific data entry requirements. Agents must complete:

- **Required fields:** Property type, list price, address, bedrooms, bathrooms, garage spaces, square footage, year built, heating/cooling type
- **Property status:** Active, Pending, Sold, Withdrawn, Expired — must be accurate at all times
- **Photo requirements:** Minimum 3 photos required; front exterior must be included
- **MLS remarks:** 500-character limit for public remarks; separate field for agent-to-agent remarks
- **Showing instructions:** Set up through ShowingTime (MIBOR's default showing management platform)
- **Open house entry:** Separate entry required for each open house event

### BLC Compliance Issues to Avoid

- Days on Market (DOM) clock starts at list date — never withdraw and relist to reset DOM without genuine reason
- MLS listings must reflect actual offered compensation (post-NAR settlement: seller-offered buyer compensation is optional and cannot be set at $0 simply to comply — if offered, it must be a real amount)
- Properties must be entered within 1 business day of signing listing agreement
- Delayed marketing must be documented with a signed waiver from the seller

## Forms Platform Access

Most Indiana agents access IAR forms through:
- **Dotloop** — integrated with MIBOR; most common in Central Indiana
- **ZipForms Plus** — IAR's designated forms platform; includes all current IAR forms
- **SkySlope** — popular transaction management platform with form integration

Contact your managing broker if you don't have access to current IAR forms.
